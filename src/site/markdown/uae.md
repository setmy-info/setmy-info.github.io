# UAE AI Hubs and Data Center Projects

The United Arab Emirates (UAE) is building AI hubs and data centers on a massive scale. This is not just a single
project, but a broad national strategy aimed at transforming the country into a global AI leader. Below are all the key
projects.

## 1. Stargate UAE – Abu Dhabi (The Largest Project)

This is the UAE's largest and most ambitious AI infrastructure project to date, developed by Abu Dhabi tech giant G42 in
collaboration with global partners including OpenAI, Oracle, NVIDIA, Cisco, and SoftBank.

- **Location:** A 5-gigawatt (GW) **UAE–US AI Campus** in the Abu Dhabi desert
- **Capacity:** The project delivers **1 GW** of computing power, making it one of the largest such clusters outside the
  United States
- **Current Status:** Construction is in full swing. The completion of the first 200 MW phase is planned for **2026**.
  Approximately 3,500 people are currently working on the site

## 2. du AI Park – Dubai (New Campus)

Dubai telecommunications company du is developing a new AI park in Dubai Silicon Oasis, expected to be completed within
five years.

- **Capacity:** The park will feature multiple liquid-cooled hyperscale data centers with a total capacity of up to **1
  GW**
- **Additional Features:** In addition to data centers, the park will include AI research laboratories, a startup
  incubator, and a national hybrid AI platform
- **Current Status:** The project is currently in the planning phase

## 3. DIEZ and VOLT Collaboration – Dubai (AI Data Center)

Also in Dubai Silicon Oasis, the Dubai Integrated Economic Zones Authority (DIEZ) is developing a new AI-ready data
center in partnership with Dutch developer VOLT.

- **Capacity:** The center will be built in two phases with a total area of up to 60,000 m². The first phase will
  provide **29 MW** of capacity, with an additional **100 MW** in the second phase, bringing the total capacity to **129
  MW**
- **Partners:** Schneider Electric is also involved in the technical implementation
- **Current Status:** The project has been recently announced

## 4. du and NextGenAI Supercluster – Dubai (Immediately Available)

In addition to long-term developments, du has also launched a ready-to-use AI supercluster in partnership with
NextGenAI.

- **Location:** Dubai Silicon Oasis data center
- **Capacity:** This **13 MW** cluster uses NVIDIA B300 GPUs equipped with direct-to-chip liquid cooling
- **Current Status:** The cluster is already **operational**, and companies and institutions can utilize its computing
  power

## Summary

The UAE is not just building a new data center; it is creating an entire AI ecosystem that encompasses both massive
campuses and immediately available capabilities. This is part of the country's strategy to diversify its economy and
reduce its dependence on oil.

# UAE AI Projects: Who Is Building AI Applications?

While all four major UAE projects are building critical AI infrastructure (data centers and computing power), two stand
out for their explicit and ambitious plans to develop AI applications and agents. A third project is designed as a
launchpad for others to build them.

This overview explains which projects are actively creating AI applications and how they fit into the UAE's national AI
strategy.

## The Key AI Application Builders

### 1. Stargate UAE (G42) - The Primary AI Agent Creator

This is the most significant player in actually building AI applications. The project's lead, Abu Dhabi tech giant G42,
has announced a very clear and massive goal.

**What They Are Building:**
G42 aims to create **one billion AI agents** throughout 2026. These are not just chatbots; they are autonomous software
systems designed to perform specific jobs.

**Examples of Applications:**
The CEO stated these agents will range from **petroleum engineers** to **cybersecurity analysts**. G42 is creating
practical, job-specific AI tools for key sectors including:

- Energy and petroleum engineering
- Cybersecurity operations
- Healthcare diagnostics
- Financial services

**Underlying Goal:**
The purpose is to boost the UAE's GDP and "export those tokens as packaged intelligence to the rest of the world." G42
is building a new industry around selling AI capabilities globally.

### 2. du AI Park - The AI Innovation Hub

This project is designed to be an **ecosystem** where AI applications are created, tested, and launched. It is a
physical and digital "place" for AI innovation.

**What They Are Building:**
The park will host:

- **AI research laboratories** for cutting-edge development
- **Start-up incubators** for new AI companies
- **National Hybrid AI platform** for secure AI deployment

**Supporting Platform:**
Alongside the physical park, du has launched a **National Hybrid AI platform** that provides government and enterprises
with an integrated set of services to **develop, deploy, and manage** AI applications securely.

**Purpose:**
The park is explicitly designed to let innovators "prototype, scale, and deploy AI solutions securely." du's CEO states
they are moving from "future AI possibilities" to "operational AI reality today."

### 3. DIEZ + VOLT (Dubai) - The "AI Factory"

While this project's primary focus is building the physical data center, its stated purpose goes beyond just providing
power and space.

**What They Are Building:**
The CEO of VOLT described the facility as more than a data center; it is designed as a potential **"AI factory"** - "a
facility where energy is transformed into intelligence."

**How It Enables Applications:**
By providing a massive, secure, and highly resilient computing platform, the facility allows nations and organizations
to **"develop, train, and deploy AI securely, and at scale."** It provides the raw computational power needed to create
complex AI models.

### 4. du + NextGenAI Supercluster - The Immediate Computing Power

This project is currently focused on providing the high-performance computing **infrastructure** that developers need to
run their AI applications.

**What It Does:**
It offers one of the region's most powerful GPU clusters (using NVIDIA B300 chips with direct-to-chip liquid cooling).
This is the engine for AI.

**How It Enables Applications:**
The cluster is designed to help enterprises, research institutions, and government entities **"accelerate the
development of AI applications and large-scale machine learning models."** Customers are already using it to gain
competitive advantages in fields such as:

- Healthcare and medical research
- Financial services and trading
- Logistics and supply chain optimization
- Government services

## The Big Picture: A National AI Strategy

It is important to see these projects as part of a massive, coordinated national effort. The UAE government itself has
launched a **national Agentic AI programme** with two main goals:

1. **Transform 50% of government services** using Agentic AI technologies
2. **Train 80,000 government employees** across all levels (from ministers to new recruits) to become experts in using
   these AI tools

This means there will be an enormous demand for the AI agents and applications that G42 and the du AI Park ecosystem are
being built to create.

## Conclusion

The UAE is not just building data centers; it is creating a complete AI ecosystem. G42 is directly building billions of
AI agents for specific industries. The du AI Park is creating a launchpad for startups and researchers. The DIEZ+VOLT
and NextGenAI projects provide the foundational computing power that makes all of this possible. Together, they form a
coordinated national strategy to make the UAE a global leader in AI application development and deployment.
