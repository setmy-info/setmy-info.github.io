## Low-voltage, electric field driven

Low-voltage, electric field driven, dry EAPs from research institutes (no commercial shops) focus on PVDF piezo films
and nanostructured ionic-dry hybrids. These are accessible via university collaborations for prototype setups.
Most Usable Research Options

### 1. PVDF Piezo Films (Dry, 9-200V electric field)

Tartu University (closest): Prof. Alvo Aabloo (alvo.aabloo@ut.ee) – they produce metallized PVDF films in their lab.
Request research samples for robot arm.

Fraunhofer IAP Germany (iap.fraunhofer.de): Low-voltage PVDF stacks for actuators – contact sensors@iap.fraunhofer.de
for collaboration samples.

Why usable: Works with 12V battery directly, dry operation, fast response (ms), established protocols.

### 2. Nanostructured Block Copolymer EAP (Dry, 1-10V)

Nature Communications research (ncomms3208): PSS-b-PMB with ionic liquids – Cambridge/LMU Munich collaboration. Email
corresponding authors for samples.

Fraunhofer IPA (ipa.fraunhofer.de): Industrial-scale dry EAP electrolytes – contact their EAP group for prototype
materials.

### 3. Hybrid Ionic-Dry EAP (Semi-dry, 2-5V)

Rajiv Gandhi Institute (India): Dr. Vipin Amoli (vamoli@rgipt.ac.in) – H-iEAPs with confined ionic liquids. Open to
international collaboration.

ETIS Estonia: Estonian research portal lists EAP projects – contact via etis.ee for local labs.

### Setup Strategy (Institute Route)

Email template: "Requesting PVDF/IPMC research samples for Estonian ......... robot."

Start with Tartu: Aabloo team already does CPC/PVDF – easiest access.

Backup: Fraunhofer IAP (Germany) ships EU-wide for research.

Most practical: PVDF from Tartu University – dry, 12V compatible, local. Email Aabloo today for samples!
