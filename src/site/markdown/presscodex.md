# Press Codex

## Information

A press codex (press code of ethics) is a voluntary self-regulatory document that defines the ethical standards
journalists and media organizations commit to follow. It covers how editorial decisions are made, how sources are
treated, and how the public interest is balanced against other considerations.

Press codices are not legally binding — they are maintained by press councils or journalist associations and enforced
through reputational and professional mechanisms such as adjudication panels.

### Common principles across press codices

* **Accuracy** — report facts correctly; correct errors promptly and transparently.
* **Right of reply** — give parties criticized in coverage an opportunity to respond.
* **Source protection** — protect the identity of confidential sources.
* **Privacy and dignity** — respect the private lives of individuals, especially those not in public roles.
* **Non-discrimination** — avoid reinforcing prejudice based on race, gender, religion, nationality, or other
  characteristics.
* **Separation of advertising and editorial** — keep commercial interests clearly distinct from editorial decisions.
* **Transparency of ownership** — disclose ownership and financial interests that could influence editorial content.
* **Independence** — editorial decisions must be independent from political, commercial, or other external pressure.

### Why it matters

Press codices provide a basis for public trust in media. Readers and sources use them to evaluate whether a
publication's practices meet a recognized standard of professional journalism. Regulators and courts sometimes
reference codex compliance when assessing editorial responsibility.
