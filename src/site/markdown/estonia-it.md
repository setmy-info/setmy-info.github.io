## Information

Estonia is internationally recognized as one of the most digitally advanced nations. The e-Estonia initiative,
launched in the late 1990s, established a "digital first" government philosophy where nearly all public services are
available online.

Key components of the Estonian digital infrastructure:

* **Digital identity** — every citizen has a national ID card with a chip enabling digital signing and authentication.
  Mobile-ID and Smart-ID provide software-based equivalents for mobile devices.
* **X-Road** — a decentralized data exchange platform that connects government and private databases. Services query
  data across registries without centralizing it in one place.
* **i-Voting** — Estonia has offered internet voting in national elections since 2005, one of the first countries to
  do so at scale.
* **e-Tax Board (e-MTA)** — the Tax and Customs Board provides a fully digital portal for filing taxes, declarations,
  and employer reports. Pre-filled tax returns are available for most residents.
* **e-Residency** — a digital identity program allowing non-residents to establish and manage EU-based companies
  using Estonian digital services remotely.
* **e-Health** — national digital health records accessible to patients and authorized healthcare providers.
* **e-School, e-Notary, e-Court** — digital portals covering education, notarial acts, and legal proceedings.

where long-running systems are kept operational without updates, prioritizing stability over modernization.
