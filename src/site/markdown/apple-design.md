# Apple Design

## Information

`Apple Design` refers to Apple's broader design resources, philosophy, and platform-facing guidance for creating
products and experiences that fit the Apple ecosystem.

In practice, this includes visual language, design assets, typography guidance, iconography, platform expectations, and
links to more specific resources such as the `Human Interface Guidelines`.

It is especially relevant when teams need an entry point into Apple's design thinking rather than only one narrow
component or one implementation library.

### Main Functionalities and Features

* **Design Resource Hub**: Serves as a central entry point to Apple's design-related documentation and assets.
* **Platform Alignment**: Helps teams understand how products should feel across Apple ecosystems.
* **System Asset Access**: Often includes references to icons, templates, typography, and platform-specific resources.
* **Connection to Guidelines**: Leads to more detailed guidance such as the `Human Interface Guidelines`.

### How `Apple Design` Fits Into Product Work

`Apple Design` is a broad umbrella concept and resource area.

It is often useful when:

* a team is starting work on Apple-platform experiences,
* designers need official Apple resources before detailed implementation,
* or product decisions need to stay aligned with the Apple ecosystem.

### Common Use Cases

* Start research for Apple-platform product design.
* Find official guidance and design assets for Apple ecosystems.
* Understand how Apple presents design principles across its platforms.
