# Accounting

## Information

**Accounting** is the systematic process of recording, classifying, summarizing, and reporting financial transactions
of an organization. It provides the financial information needed for management decisions, tax compliance, and
stakeholder reporting.

Key accounting branches:

* **Financial accounting** — preparation of external financial statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow).
* **Management accounting** — internal reports for operational decision-making.
* **Tax accounting** — compliance with tax laws and preparation of tax declarations.

### Core accounting concepts

* **Double-entry bookkeeping** — every transaction has equal debit and credit entries.
* **Accrual basis** — revenue and expenses are recorded when they occur, not when cash changes hands.
* **Cash basis** — revenue and expenses recorded only when cash is received or paid.
* **Chart of accounts** — structured list of all accounts used by an organization.

## Estonian accounting

Estonian accounting is governed by the **Accounting Act (Raamatupidamise seadus)** and the Estonian Generally
Accepted Accounting Principles (Estonian GAAP), published by the Accounting Standards Board
(Raamatupidamise Toimkond, RTK). Larger companies may also prepare IFRS-compliant statements.

Key requirements for Estonian entities:

* Every registered legal entity must keep accounts.
* Annual reports must be submitted to the **Business Register** within 6 months of the financial year end.
* Bookkeeping may be done in Estonian or another language, but the annual report must be in Estonian.
* Source documents must be retained for **7 years**.

### Key Estonian tax declarations related to accounting

* **TSD** — monthly employer tax declaration (income and social tax). See [tsd.md](tsd.md).
* **KMD** — VAT return (käibemaksudeklaratsioon), monthly or quarterly.
* **INF** — various informational declarations to EMTA.

### Software commonly used in Estonia

* Directo
* Merit Aktiva
* Netvisor
* SAP / Microsoft Dynamics for larger companies

## Usage, tips and tricks

* Always keep source documents (invoices, contracts, bank statements) for 7 years.
* Reconcile bank accounts monthly to catch discrepancies early.
* Use separate bank accounts for business and personal transactions.
* For VAT-registered entities, file KMD on time — late filing incurs penalties.
