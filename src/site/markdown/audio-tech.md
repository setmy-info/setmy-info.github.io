# Audio tech

## Information

This page is a reference for audio equipment used in home recording, live sound, and content creation setups. It
covers microphones, mixers, audio interfaces, stands, and accessories.

## Usage, tips and tricks

### Microphone placement

* Use the close-mic technique (5–20 cm from the source) to capture a dry, direct sound with minimal room reflection.
* Point the microphone slightly off-axis (at an angle) to reduce plosives and sibilance on dynamic mics.
* Avoid placing the microphone directly in front of a speaker or monitor to prevent feedback.

### Gain staging

* Set the preamp gain so the loudest signal peaks around -18 dBFS to leave headroom.
* Check for clipping at every stage: preamp, interface, DAW track.
* A consistent noise floor matters more than maximum gain.

### Monitoring levels

* Mix at moderate listening levels (around 75–85 dB SPL) to reduce ear fatigue.
* Reference your mix through different speakers (headphones, laptop, phone) before finalizing.
